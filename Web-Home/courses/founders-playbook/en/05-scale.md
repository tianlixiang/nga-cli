# 05. Scale Stage — From Solo to Agentic Team

> **Exit criteria**: Key business flows run when the founder is absent, and the team (humans + agents) ships weekly.

Scale is no longer "founder + AI" — it's "founder + team + AI." Here you hit the counterintuitive truth of AI-native companies: **your first hire might not be an engineer**. Because AI maxed out engineering capacity, the bottleneck shifts elsewhere — sales, customer success, operations.

## Stage Goals

1. **Switch from "founder heroics" to repeatable processes**
2. **Decide what to hire humans for vs. agents for**
3. **Build a sustainable financial model** (no longer funding-dependent)
4. **Protect PMF** — Scale is when PMF is most easily lost

## Hire People vs. Hire Agents

For each new work category, ask three questions:

1. **Is it judgment-intensive or execution-intensive?**
   - Execution → agent
   - Judgment → human
2. **Does it require customer trust?**
   - Yes → human (key sales, customer success)
   - No → agent
3. **What's the cost of being wrong?**
   - High (contracts, product direction) → human
   - Low (drafts, data wrangling) → agent

### Typical split

| Work type | Who | Why |
|-----------|-----|-----|
| Sales email drafts | Agent | Execution-heavy, low cost of error |
| Annual contract negotiation | Human (founder / sales lead) | High cost + trust |
| Technical documentation | Agent + engineer review | Agent drafts, human verifies |
| Customer onboarding | Human (B2B) / Agent (B2C) | B2B needs trust, B2C doesn't |
| Data dashboards | Agent + engineer reviews architecture | Execution-heavy |
| Hiring interviews | Human | Judgment + inverted trust |

## Financial Model

Scale requires switching to "revenue-driven," no more funding life support:

### Three numbers to watch weekly

1. **MRR**: month-over-month growth, target ≥ 15%
2. **CAC payback**: ≤ 12 months
3. **Cash runway**: ≥ 12 months

### AI-native financial advantage

| Item | Traditional SaaS | AI-Native |
|------|-----------------|-----------|
| Engineer headcount | High | Low (AI replaces) |
| CS headcount | High | Medium (partial agent) |
| Marketing content output | Slow + costly | Fast + cheap |
| Gross margin | 70-80% | 80-90% |

**+10% gross margin is huge** — same revenue, more reinvestment into product and sales.

## Protect PMF

Biggest hidden risk in Scale: **you think you're scaling existing PMF, but it's quietly slipping away**.

### Three early signals

1. **New-cohort retention dropping** (D30 from 40% to 30%)
2. **Support ticket category shifting** (from "how to use" to "why designed this way")
3. **Founder no longer personally meeting customers** (most dangerous)

### How to defend

- **Founder meets ≥ 5 customers/week**, regardless of company size
- **Weekly feedback walkthrough**: agent triages, founder personally reads top 10
- **No scaling for scaling's sake** — if growth lags team growth, contract

## Common Failure Modes

| Failure | Why | Fix |
|---------|-----|-----|
| **Premature senior engineer hires** | "We need to scale engineering" | AI replaced 80%, hire sales/CS first |
| **Founder steps back to 'strategy'** | "Need to think big picture" | Not yet — keep meeting customers |
| **Funding-driven** | "One more round" | Scale requires revenue-driven |
| **Copying Silicon Valley playbook** | "OpenAI/Anthropic do it this way" | You're not them. You're you. |
| **Agents running unsupervised** | Full automation, no human gate | Critical decisions need humans |

## Exit Checklist

- [ ] MRR MoM ≥ 15% (6+ consecutive months)
- [ ] CAC payback ≤ 12 months
- [ ] Cash runway ≥ 12 months
- [ ] Key flows documented, new hires onboard in 1 week
- [ ] Founder still meets ≥ 5 customers/week
- [ ] D30 retention hasn't dropped

If 2+ items fail, **go back to Launch** and polish. Scale is an outcome, not a target.
