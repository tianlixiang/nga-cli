# 02. Idea Stage — Validate the Problem Hypothesis

> **Exit criteria**: You can name 10+ specific customers who would pay, and their problem descriptions converge.

The fastest way to die in the Idea stage isn't failing to come up with an idea — it's **falling in love with an idea nobody wants**. AI cuts the workload here by an order of magnitude, but it won't judge "is this worth doing." It just makes you go faster. Faster in the wrong direction means dying faster.

## Stage Goals

1. **Find a real problem** — not "I think people would want this," but "I've seen the same pain in N independent scenarios"
2. **Map the competition** — what exists, why isn't it good enough, did AI redefine "good enough"?
3. **Identify the first paying-ready persona** — not "all SaaS users," but "X-industry Y-roles bothered weekly by Z"

## Three AI-Powered Exercises

### Exercise 1: Problem-Hypothesis Canvas

Have Claude help you write 20 one-line problem hypotheses in the structure:

> "**[Who]** doing **[what task]** hits **[what pain]**, currently workarounds it with **[what kludge]**, paying a cost of **[how much time/money]**."

Force yourself to fill all 20. The first 5 are from your head; entries 6+ are where you start challenging assumptions.

### Exercise 2: Batch Interview Scripts

Old way: 3 interviews/week, hand-recorded.
New way: have Claude generate 20 industries × 3 roles × 5 questions of scripts; you pick the 5 most relevant to actually run.

**Note**: AI-generated scripts are the *start*, not the *end*. Real insights come from follow-up questions — and follow-ups must be asked by a human.

### Exercise 3: Competitive Map

Run with Claude:
1. List every product solving the problem (direct + indirect + DIY)
2. For each: core promise / pricing / biggest weakness
3. Mark the "impossible before AI, possible after" gaps

Those gaps are your opening.

## Common Failure Modes

| Failure | Why | Fix |
|---------|-----|-----|
| **Only talking to willing listeners** | Strong survivor bias | Force 2 "obvious non-buyers" per week |
| **Confusing "nice" with "will pay"** | These are different things | Ask "would you buy at $120/yr?" directly |
| **Falling for the tech early** | Classic engineer trap | No IDE for the first 30 days |
| **Competitive map as SWOT** | SWOT doesn't drive decisions | Use "gap × price tier" 2D matrix instead |
| **Expecting AI to judge direction** | AI has no taste | AI generates, you curate |

## Exit Checklist

- [ ] I can explain the problem in 30 seconds
- [ ] I have 10+ named target customers
- [ ] I know their current workaround and its cost
- [ ] I can name the "impossible-before-AI" differentiator
- [ ] 3+ customers verbally committed to try/pay

If any answer is "kind of," **do not move to MVP**. Go talk to 10 more people.
